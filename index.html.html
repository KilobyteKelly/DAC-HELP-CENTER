Analytics Dashboard
Your central view for cost, activity, and usage data

The Dashboard is your analytics home base. It shows you high-level cost and activity data across your entire deployment, with charts you can filter and drill into. Everything updates based on the billing cycle and filters you select.

What you'll see on the Dashboard

Your Dashboard default view includes two pages you can switch between:

Dashboard
Top-level widgets showing cost and activity data cross-referenced across your deployment. A good daily check-in view.
Usage
An all-in-one view for data, SMS, and voice usage plus SIM utilization — current billing cycle and historical trends.
Navigating between pages
  1. Go to the Analytics section in the portal's main navigation.
  2. Select the page tab you want — Dashboard or Usage — at the top of the screen.
  3. Charts load automatically based on your current filter settings.
💡
Good to knowMost charts are interactive — click on a legend item to show or hide that metric. This lets you zero in on specific carriers, customers, or technologies without leaving the page.
Adjusting what data you see

Session Filters let you narrow the dashboard to exactly the data you need. Changes apply across all widgets on the current page until you reset them.

  1. Look for the Session Filters area at the top of any analytics page.
  2. Choose your Carrier Account, Customer, or Technology to narrow the scope.
  3. Select your preferred data unit: GB, MB, or KB.
  4. Use the sliding bar to select the billing cycle time frame (up to 12 months back).
  5. Select Apply to update all charts with your selections.
ℹ️
Currency filtersIf your deployment spans multiple currencies, a currency filter will appear automatically in the Session Filters panel.
Printing or saving a dashboard view
  1. Set up your dashboard filters and page view exactly as you want them to appear.
  2. Select the ··· (more options) button next to any report tab.
  3. Choose Print PDF from the menu.
  4. Confirm the download in the pop-up dialog — your PDF will save to your computer.
💡
TipApply your session filters before printing to capture only the data range and carrier/customer view you want in the PDF.

Usage Analytics
Data, SMS, voice usage and SIM utilization at a glance

The Usage page is your all-in-one hub for understanding how your devices are consuming data, SMS, and voice. You can see both the current billing cycle and the full history of your deployment side by side.

What's shown on this page
  • Current cycle-to-date usage for data, SMS, and voice
  • SIM utilization percentages — how much of your pool is actively being used
  • Historical usage trends across past billing cycles
  • Inactivity monitoring — SIMs that haven't connected recently
Reading the charts

Each widget on the Usage page can be filtered independently using the chart's built-in metric toggles. Click any legend item to show or hide it. This is especially useful for comparing specific carriers or customer segments without changing your session filters.

ℹ️
Usage vs. DashboardThe Dashboard page gives you a cost-and-activity summary. The Usage page focuses specifically on consumption metrics. Use both together for the full picture.

Device Details & Actions
Find devices, view their profiles, and take action

Device Management is where you search for individual SIMs, review their full profile, and perform carrier actions like activating, deactivating, or changing rate plans — for one device or many at once.

Three ways to search
Quick Search
Use the funnel icon to open Quick Search. Search by any device ID type (ICCID, MSISDN, IMEI, IMSI, EID, or Custom Columns) and filter by Customer, Status, or Rate Plan.
List Search
Search for up to 2,000 devices at once by entering comma-separated device IDs. Ideal for bulk lookups or comparisons.
Column Filters
Click the Columns button in the results table to toggle which fields are displayed. Save your column preference for future sessions.
Default columns in your device list

These columns appear by default when viewing search results. You can add, remove, or reorder them using the Columns button.

Internal Device ID External Device ID ICCID MSISDN/MDN IMEI IMSI Status CTD Usage (MB) Customer Name Activation Date InSession Indicator
Viewing a full device profile

Click on any device's IMEI in the results table to open its full profile. The profile includes:

  • Profile summary and capability overview
  • Connectivity and network information
  • Hardware details and specifications
  • Programming and router details
  • Audio and voice-over details
Acting on one or a few devices
  1. Search for and locate your device(s) in the results table.
  2. Check the box next to each device you want to act on.
  3. Select the action you want from the Actions drop-down menu.
  4. Fill in any required information (e.g., a new rate plan name).
  5. Confirm to process the action.
Acting on many devices at once (Bulk)
  1. Select the action you want from the Actions drop-down.
  2. Download the template spreadsheet that appears.
  3. Fill in your SIM/device data in the template.
  4. Import and upload the completed template back into the portal.
  5. Verify the job status on the Action Status page (if available in your plan).
Available actions

Available actions depend on your carrier connection. Common actions include:

  • Activate SIM
  • Deactivate SIM
  • Suspend SIM
  • Restore SIM
  • Change Rate Plan
  • Terminate / Kill SIM
  • Send SMS
⚠️
Heads up on Terminate / Kill SIMThis action is permanent and cannot be undone. Double-check your selection before confirming.

Ad-Hoc Report Builder
Build, analyze, and schedule custom reports on your data

The Ad-Hoc Report Builder lets you construct custom reports using filters you define. Build them once and re-run them anytime, export to Excel or CSV, and even schedule automatic delivery. No coding required.

Five report categories to choose from
Category What it contains
Usage by Device All device-level usage data for a specific connection, including usage statistics per SIM.
Cost Allocation Usage and cost data for devices, organized per billing cycle.
Monthly Usage History Usage data on devices per billing cycle — the go-to for optimization analysis.
Usage by Zone Tracking Usage combined with zone data per billing cycle. Note: devices active in multiple zones may appear on multiple lines. Not available on all connections.
Change Tracking by Device Records of changes made to devices (e.g., rate plan changes, status changes). Availability depends on carrier data.
Building your first report
  1. Go to Reports & Notifications in the portal navigation, then select Ad-Hoc Reports.
  2. Click Add New Report from the left-side menu.
  3. Give your report a name and optional description.
  4. Click + Add Filter to set your query parameters — choose a column, set the operator (greater than, includes, equals, etc.), and enter a value.
  5. Add as many filters as you need, then click Save.
💡
Start simpleBuild your first report with just one or two filters. Once you see results you can refine further. Saved reports can always be edited.
Three ways to look at your results
Preview
Shows the first 5 rows of your report results — a quick sanity check before exporting. Click the Excel icon to export the full report to a spreadsheet.
Analyze
A customizable pivot table view with multiple chart types. Mix dimensions and measures to slice your data any way you need. Changes update in real time as you configure.
History
Shows a log of every time this report has been run — including who received it, when, and whether it succeeded. Useful for confirming scheduled reports are running.
Setting up automatic delivery

Once a report is saved, you can schedule it to run and deliver automatically on a recurring basis.

  1. Open the report and go to its Settings.
  2. Select the Schedule tab.
  3. Choose your frequency: Daily, Weekly, or Monthly.
  4. Set the time you want the report to run.
  5. Choose the export format: Excel or CSV.
  6. Save your schedule settings.
Other report settings
General
Update the report description, toggle it active or inactive, set an expiration date, or adjust priority.
Permissions
Control which other portal users can view or edit this report.
Actions
Configure automatic device actions to fire when devices match your report's criteria — for example, automatically change rate plans for devices exceeding a usage threshold.
ℹ️
Report ActionsThe Actions tab lets you automate device changes based on report results. This is a powerful feature — if you're unsure, contact DAC support before setting up automated actions.
